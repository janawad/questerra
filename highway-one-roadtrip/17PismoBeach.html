Butterfly Grove,
Pismo Beach

At the mid-point between San Francisco and Los Angeles, Pismo Beach was once the Clam Capital of the World. These days, each January it plays host to thousands of migratory monarch butterflies which come to mate in the eucalyptus groves. Why there? Scientists are baffled as to why the monarchs return to the same spot each year, they think perhaps the butterflies have a pretty sophisticated genetic homing system. And to the south, you'll find the famous Oceano Dunes, where you can go dune-bashing in 4x4s and dune buggies.
