Alcatraz

Between 1934 and 1963, this was America's most notorious prison. A stretch in here would be torture, made all the worse because from behind these bars, you could hear the sound of laughter and chatter drifting over from the mainland. It was said to be escape-proof - many have tried but it seems none have succeeded.

In 1962, prisoner John Paul Scott greased himself thoroughly with lard, squeezed through a window and attempted to swim to shore. Police found him lying at the foot of the iconic Golden Gate Bridge unconscious in hypothermic shock. Infamous mob boss Al Capone was amongst the very first inmates in August 1934 and was a founding member of the Alcatraz prison band.
