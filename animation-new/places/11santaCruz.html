Santa Cruz

Whatever those bozos over at Huntingdon Beach say, Santa Cruz is the real Surf City: Steamer Lane is the birthplace of surfing. And it's held onto its laid-back, beach bum vibes since the seventies. This town still runs off flower power, still rides its own wave. Down by the water there's the legendary vintage boardwalk with its historic wooden roller coaster, the Giant Dipper, that affords a bird's eye view of the Pacific. And the region's also famous for the none-more-liberal University of California, which nestles amongst a coastal giant redwood forest.
